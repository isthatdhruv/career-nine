✔ Career Guidance Gr 6–12 — NEP §15.6

📌NEP 2020 mandates age-appropriate career counselling from Grade 6 onward as a continuous journey, not a one-time test. The policy explicitly moves away from stream-selection pressure in Grade 10–11 toward gradual, informed career awareness built year by year.
What the policy requires
  • Career exposure and exploration activities for Grades 6, 7, and 8 — age-appropriate, interest-based
  • Subject-awareness counselling in Grades 9–10 to inform stream selection
  • Professional career guidance and college readiness support in Grades 11–12
  • Counsellor-to-student ratio: recommended 1:150 by NEP (CBSE guideline)
  • Documentation of each student's career journey — updated annually
How your school complies
Navigator 360 is active across all three stages: Insight Navigator (Gr 6–8) builds self-awareness through interests, values, and learning styles; Subject Navigator (Gr 9–10) links learning styles to subject selection; Career Navigator (Gr 11–12) provides suitability scoring and college readiness guidance. All 487 students are covered. Digital records are maintained per student in the Counsellor dashboard.

✔ Holistic Progress Assessment — NEP §4.37 & CBSE HP Card

📌NEP 2020 §4.37 requires that assessment move beyond marks and grades to capture the whole child — including character, competencies, creativity, and collaboration. CBSE's Holistic Progress Card (introduced 2023) operationalises this for Classes 3–8 and is being extended through Class 12.
What the policy requires
  • Assessment across cognitive, emotional, social, and physical domains
  • No ranking or comparison between students — individual growth tracking
  • Student self-assessment and peer feedback components
  • Portfolio-based evidence in addition to written examinations
  • 360-degree feedback involving teachers, parents, and the student
How your school complies
Navigator 360's 6-pillar framework directly maps to the CBSE Holistic Progress Card parameters. The six pillars — Career Personality, Learning Styles (MI), Ability, Values, Subjects of Interest, and Aspirations — capture cognitive (Ability, MI), character (Values), and aspirational (Aspirations, Personality) dimensions. Reports are student-specific, non-comparative, and updated each assessment cycle. Teacher dashboards provide individual growth tracking per student.

✔ Multiple Intelligences — NEP §4.6 & Howard Gardner Model

📌NEP 2020 §4.6 explicitly states that the traditional focus on linguistic and logical intelligences must give way to recognition of diverse forms of intelligence. Schools are directed to identify and nurture each child's unique intelligence profile across arts, sports, languages, music, and other domains.
What the policy requires
  • Recognition that every child has a unique combination of strengths — not just academic ability
  • Classroom strategies tailored to diverse learning styles, not one-size-fits-all teaching
  • Extracurricular integration for students strong in artistic, kinesthetic, musical, and naturalistic domains
  • Teacher training in identifying and supporting non-traditional intelligences
  • Parent communication about their child's specific intelligence profile
How your school complies & what the data shows
All 8 Gardner intelligences are assessed per student. The Teacher Dashboard auto-generates class-level learning style profiles and recommends specific teaching strategies. Notable finding: Bodily-Kinesthetic intelligence is the highest in Grades 6–8 (72%), yet receives the least classroom attention — prime area for growth. Linguistic intelligence is dropping across grades (71% → 58%) indicating a curriculum gap.
Action recommended: Schedule MI-based parent communication sessions this term. Share each child's top 3 intelligence areas with parents to align home support. Teacher training on differentiated instruction needed for Musical and Linguistic domains — both declining across grades.

✔ CBSE Career Guidance Cell — CBSE Circular 2023

📌CBSE's 2023 circular mandates every affiliated school to establish a functional Career Guidance Cell with a designated trained counsellor, student-accessible resources, documented session records, and integration with the school's academic calendar.
What the policy requires
  • Designated school counsellor trained in career guidance (CBSE-approved certification)
  • Minimum 2 career guidance sessions per student per year — documented
  • Career resource library accessible to students (physical or digital)
  • Annual Career Fair or Career Day event
  • Collaboration with external colleges, universities, and industry for student exposure
  • Individual student counselling records maintained for audit
  • Parent involvement in at least 1 career session per year
How your school complies
The Counsellor Dashboard provides individual student profiles, session note tracking, cross-role communication (teacher → counsellor → principal), and searchable student records filterable by grade, section, personality type, and CCI status. All session notes are date-stamped and per-student. Red-flag alerts from teachers arrive directly in the counsellor's message inbox.
Recommended next steps: (1) Schedule the Annual Career Fair — no date set yet. (2) Document parent involvement sessions in the counsellor records. (3) Verify counsellor's CBSE certification is current. (4) Ensure career resource library (physical or Career-9 digital) is communicated to all students.

⚠ Vocational Integration — NEP §16 & CBSE Skill Education Framework

⚠Current status: 34% of students exposed to vocational fields. NEP target: 50% by 2025, 100% by 2030. This is the most significant compliance gap in your school's NEP implementation.
What the policy requires
  • At least 50% of all students must have exposure to at least one vocational skill by 2025
  • Vocational education must be treated as equal to academic education — not a fallback option
  • CBSE Skill subjects available from Class 6: IT, Agriculture, Home Science, Retail, Healthcare, Beauty & Wellness, Tourism, BFSI
  • 10-day vocational internship for students in Classes 6–8 (bagless day format)
  • NSQF-aligned certification for students completing skill modules in Grades 9–12
  • Integration into timetable — not only as an add-on but as a regular subject option
What your Navigator data reveals — and what to do

  • Naturalistic intelligence students (70% in Gr 6–8) have high Agriculture / Environmental suitability, yet fewer than 10% aspire to those fields

⚠ Experiential Learning & Field Trips — NEP §4.6, §7.9

⚠No experiential field trips scheduled this academic year. NEP mandates a minimum of 10 bagless days per year. CBSE requires career-exploration events in the Annual Activity Calendar.
What the policy requires
  • Minimum 10 bagless days per year — hands-on, activity-based learning inside or outside school
  • At least 2 career-exploration visits per academic year per grade group
  • Annual Activity Calendar submitted to CBSE must include career-linked field trip dates
  • Community engagement activities — local craftspeople, farmers, healthcare workers visiting school
  • Reflection documentation: what did students learn? How did it change their understanding?
Profile-matched trip planning — why counsellor involvement is mandatory
Generic tours (e.g., "everyone visits a factory") have minimal career-clarity impact. Navigator data shows that students with Medical aspiration need to visit a hospital; Realistic students need to visit a manufacturing plant; Artistic students need an art studio or media house. A counsellor-planned trip, matched to class profiles, produces measurably higher CCI improvement in the following assessment cycle.
🗺 Navigator-Aligned Trip Ideas
Gr 6–8: Science museum, agricultural farm, art gallery, newspaper printing press, crafts centre

Gr 9–10: Hospital / medical college, IT company campus, court visit, college open days, engineering workshop

Gr 11 Science: Research institute, pharmacy, IIT/NIT open day, environmental field station, biotech lab

Gr 11 Commerce/Arts: Stock exchange, ad agency, art institute, NGO headquarters, hotel management college
🧠 Counsellor's Role — 3-Step Process
Step 1 — Before the trip: Counsellor reviews class Navigator data, identifies which students benefit most, selects destination, conducts 15-min orientation session connecting the visit to personal profiles.

Step 2 — During the trip: Counsellor provides each student a 3-question observation card linked to their top career match. Encourages them to look for specific things.

Step 3 — After the trip: Counsellor facilitates 20-min reflection, updates aspiration notes, records CCI shifts. Tracks whether the visit raised, lowered, or clarified aspirations.
